Humba

Description

Humba is a Filipino dish that originally came from the islands' contact with Chinese traders. This delicious dish consists of tender pork, black beans, and spices. Some versions of humba also contain banana chunks, which is my personal favorite.

If you travel to the Philippines and are invited to a party, wedding, birthday, or any other type of get-together, you can be sure you will find humba on the serving table. If you don't want to wait to be served this amazing dish, read below to learn how you can make it yourself!

Ingredients

  • Pork belly
  • Pineapple juice
  • Salted black beans
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Banana blossoms
  • Dried bay leaves
  • Peppercorn
  • Brown sugar

Steps

  1. Cook pork belly in a pot until it browns
  2. Add garlic and onion, cook until onion softens
  3. Add two tablespoons of soy sauce, pepper corn, and bay leaves
  4. Add one cup pineapple juice and cook with pork until it boils
  5. Let it simmer for about 30-40 mins
  6. Add two tablespoons of vinegar and let it reboil
  7. Add black beans while humba simmers
  8. Add in sugar to taste
  9. Cook all ingredients together for 5-10 mins
