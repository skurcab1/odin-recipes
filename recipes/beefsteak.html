Beef Steak

Description

Beef steak, or bistek, is an easy-to-make staple of the Filipino kitchen. Basically it is beef well done with sauted onions and garlic. This dish originated with the Spanish after their first contact with the Philippine islands. Cows are not endemic to the islands, but thankfully they were brought here and now we can enjoy a nice steak, Filipino style!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef sirlion
  • 4 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1/4 tablespoon of ground black pepper
  • 3 yellow onion
  • 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 4 tablespoons of your preferred cooking oil
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 cup of water

Steps

  1. Marinate steak for one hour in black pepper and lemon
  2. Fry some of the onions in oil and put in a separate dish
  3. After draining the marinade from the beef, fry the beef for 1 min on each side
  4. Saute garlic and remaining onions
  5. Bring remaining marinade to a boil and add in the beef, onions, and garlic
  6. Simmer until meat is tender
  7. Season as needed, and add fried onions
