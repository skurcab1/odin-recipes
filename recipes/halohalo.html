Halo Halo

Description

If you're in the mood to try a Filipino desert, the halo halo is a must. The name is Tagalog for "mixed," and that is exactly what the halo halo is: a mixture of various sweet things. Inside a halo halo you will find ice cream, coconut, banana, bits of flan, beans (?!) and everything else delectable and cavity-inducing.

No matter where you go in the Philippines, no matter what restaurant, from Jollibee to a street vendor, you'll find the national favorite, the halo halo. Visitors to the Philippines may initially be taken aback at such a creative mixing of various (seemingly random) ingredients, but once you try one, you'll be sure to understand the genius of this dessert.

Ingredients

  • ube ice cream
  • flan
  • sweetend beans
  • coconut gel
  • pinipig
  • coconut strips
  • banana chunks
  • evaporated milk
  • crushed ice
  • sweetened red munggo
  • tapioca pearls

Steps (super easy!)

  1. Combine a teaspoon of all ingredients
  2. Fill a glass halfway with shaved ice
  3. Pour evaporated milk over the top
  4. Add the ice cream
  5. Serve with a long spoon
